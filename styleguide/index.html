01  Foundation

From auto finance to a banking institution

The Champion look — a dark navy world lit by electric blue, with red as a spark of energy — reads as confident and modern. A bank keeps that confidence but shifts the emphasis: legibility over spectacle, restraint over energy, and disclosure as a first-class design element.

  • Keep the navy-glass core. The deep-navy ground and frosted panels already signal security and premium seriousness — ideal for banking.
  • Demote red from decoration to signal. On the marketing site red is brand energy. In a banking product, red must mean one thing: money out, errors, or destructive actions. Never style a neutral button or heading red.
  • Add the money vocabulary the auto site lacks: semantic positive/negative colors, tabular figures, and status pills for transaction and application states.
  • Hold a WCAG 2.1 AA floor. Financial services carry real ADA/accessibility exposure; every text-on-surface pairing in this guide is contrast-checked below.
Recommendation

Split the brand into two registers. Use the expressive, high-motion navy-glass treatment for marketing and acquisition pages; use a calmer, denser variant (less glow, tighter spacing, more tabular data) for the authenticated banking app. Same tokens, different intensity.

03  Semantic color

The money vocabulary to add

The auto-finance palette has no green and no amber — a banking product cannot express balances, gains, pending states, or warnings without them. These two additions complete the system and are tuned to sit naturally beside the existing blues.

Recommendation

Never encode meaning by color alone. ~1 in 12 men has a red/green deficiency — a bank cannot let color be the only difference between a credit and a debit. Always pair the hue with a sign (+ / −), an icon, or a text label. The ledger and pills below follow this rule.

Sora (display) carries headings, balances, and brand moments with a tight −0.02em tracking. Recommendation · figures

Apply font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums to every balance, rate, account number, and table column so digits align vertically and don't jitter as values update. This is the single highest-impact typographic rule for a banking UI.

06  Trust & compliance

Disclosure is a design element

A bank's credibility lives in the fine print, the security cues, and the regulatory marks. Give them a considered, consistent treatment instead of an afterthought footer.

◆ Do

  • Set disclosures in Ink Faint at ≥14px, 1.7 line-height — legible, not buried.
  • Give FDIC/NCUA, Equal Housing, and licensing marks a fixed footer lockup.
  • Show a lock glyph + "Secured" only on genuinely encrypted screens.
  • State APR, APY, and fee terms adjacent to the number they qualify.

✕ Don't

  • Shrink legal text below 12px or drop it under 4.5:1 contrast.
  • Use Champion Red to "warn" on routine disclosures — it cries wolf.
  • Imply guaranteed approval; Champion is a partner, not the lender.
  • Animate or auto-dismiss anything a regulator expects the user to read.
Recommendation

Build a "disclosure" text style into the type scale (Inter, 14px, Ink Faint, 1.7lh, max ~70ch) and a reusable regulatory footer lockup. Standardizing these once keeps every product surface audit-ready and on-brand.

07  Motion & accessibility

Calm, honest motion

  • Ease with intent. Reuse the site's cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1) at 150–200ms for hovers and transitions. Save the explosive logo intro for marketing only — keep the app quiet.
  • Honor prefers-reduced-motion. Disable non-essential transitions and never animate balances or amounts changing (it undermines trust in the figure).
  • Visible focus everywhere. A 2px Electric Blue focus ring at 3px offset on every interactive element — banking users lean heavily on keyboards and screen readers.
  • Tap targets ≥44px and body text ≥16px, so account holders across all ages and abilities can transact confidently.
08  Voice & tone

Plain, direct, human

Champion's marketing voice is "real people, straight answers." In banking that becomes: precise about money, warm about people, never hype.

◆ Say

  • "Your transfer is scheduled for Jul 12."
  • "We couldn't verify that routing number. Check the 9 digits and try again."
  • "No monthly fee. No minimum balance."

✕ Avoid

  • "Oops! Something went wrong."
  • "Unlock exclusive wealth-building opportunities!"
  • Jargon like "ACH origination window" in customer-facing copy.
